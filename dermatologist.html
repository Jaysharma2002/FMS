5 Dermatologist Skin Care Tips

1. Use Sunscreen Daily

UV rays can cause premature aging, wrinkles, and increase the risk of skin cancer. Apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen with at least SPF 30 every day, even on cloudy days, to protect your skin from harmful sun exposure.

2. Moisturize Regularly

Keeping your skin hydrated is essential to prevent dryness, flakiness, and irritation. Choose a moisturizer suitable for your skin type (oily, dry, combination) and apply it daily, especially after washing your face or showering.

3. Avoid Over-Exfoliating

While exfoliation helps remove dead skin cells, over-exfoliating can irritate and damage the skin barrier, leading to dryness and sensitivity. Limit exfoliation to 1-2 times a week, using gentle products, particularly if you have sensitive skin.

4. Maintain a Healthy Diet

What you eat impacts your skin. Dermatologists recommend a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and healthy fats (like omega-3s) to promote glowing skin. Avoid excessive sugar and processed foods, as they can contribute to acne and inflammation.

5. Don't Pick at Your Skin

Picking at acne, blemishes, or scabs can lead to scarring, infection, and worsen the problem. It's best to resist the urge and consult a dermatologist for professional treatment options if you experience persistent issues.
